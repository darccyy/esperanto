Consonants

Most consonants are pronounced the same as in English: b, d, f, g (as in 'go'), h, k, l, m, n, p, s, t, v, z

Different pronunciations

Some different pronunciations of letters are:

  • c : like ts, as in 'pizza'
  • j : like y, as in 'yellow'

New letters

New letters, with circumflexes (a 'hat' on the letter, such as ĉ), are:

  • ĉ : like ch, as in cheese
  • ĝ : like j, as in 'judge'
  • ĥ: like the 'harsh' (velar) h or ch, as in 'loch'
  • ĵ: like zh, as in 'vision'
  • ŝ: like sh, as in 'shadow'

The letter ŭ (u with a breve) is almost always used after a, e, or o, as a diphthong. It is often considered a vowel, but included in this chart for simplicity. It is rarely used as a consonant, such as the word 'ŭaŭ' which means 'wow', and is pronounced the same.
